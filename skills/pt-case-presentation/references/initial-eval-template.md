# Initial Evaluation Presentation Template

Use this template when the user wants a first presentation based on the initial evaluation.

## 1. Basic Information

- Diagnosis
- Age and sex if provided
- Onset date or post-operative day/week
- Referral reason or presentation context

## 2. Medical and Social Background

- History of present illness
- Past medical history
- Relevant precautions, contraindications, or physician orders
- Living situation, family support, home environment, work or school role

## 3. Chief Complaint and Needs

- Main complaint
- Patient hope or stated goal
- Therapist-identified needs

## 4. Current Function

- Bed mobility, transfers, gait, stairs, upper limb use, or other relevant ADL
- Participation restrictions
- Assistive devices or orthoses

## 5. Subjective and Objective Findings

- Pain
- Range of motion
- Strength
- Balance
- Sensation
- Endurance
- Disease-specific findings
- Standardized tests if available

## 6. Problem List

- List impairments
- List activity limitations
- List participation restrictions
- Prioritize the top problems that explain the current disability

## 7. Clinical Interpretation

- Explain how the key findings connect
- Identify the likely main factors limiting function
- Clarify why those factors matter for the target ADL or participation goal

## 8. Goals

- Short-term goals
- Long-term goals
- Make goals functional, measurable, and time-aware where possible

## 9. Treatment Policy

- State the treatment priorities
- Explain the overall strategy
- Note precautions or intensity limits

## 10. Treatment Program

- Intervention item
- Purpose of the intervention
- Dosage or progression idea if the source allows it
- Expected change linked to the goal

## 11. Prognosis

- Expected improvement
- Factors supporting recovery
- Factors limiting recovery

## 12. Presentation Closing

- One-paragraph summary suitable for an in-hospital initial case conference
- Optional short speaker script if requested
